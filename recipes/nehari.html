Nehari

Description

Nehari is a flavorful, slow-cooked meat stew from the Indian subcontinent, famous for its melt-in-your-mouth tender beef or mutton.

It is prepared in a thick, rich gravy, often enhanced with bone marrow, and seasoned with a blend of aromatic spices. Traditionally

served for breakfast, this celebratory dish is typically enjoyed with naan and garnished with fresh ginger, cilantro, and lemon.

Ingredients

  • Beef or mutton shank, with bones
  • Ghee or oil
  • Onions
  • Ginger and garlic paste
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Whole wheat flour (atta) or all-purpose flour
  • Nehari masala (pre mixed spice blend)
  • Fried Onions
  • Chopped green chilies
  • Lemon wedges
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Julienned fresh ginger

Steps

  1. Heat Ghee or oil in a large pot.
  2. Add sliced onions and cook them while stirring, until they turn golden brown in color.
  3. Put in the ginger and garlic paste. Cook for 1 minute, stirring so it doesn't burn.
  4. Add the meat shank pieces. Cook and stir until the meat changes color from red to brown.
